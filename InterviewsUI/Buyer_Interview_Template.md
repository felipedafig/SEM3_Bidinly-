### Pre-Interview Instructions

**Instructions to read to the user:**

"Thank you for participating in this user interview. Today, we'll be testing the Bidinly property bidding platform from a buyer's perspective. I'll ask you to complete some tasks while thinking aloud - please share what you're thinking, what you're looking for, and any confusion or concerns you have. There are no right or wrong answers - we're testing the interface, not you. Feel free to be honest about your experience. Do you have any questions before we begin?"

### Scenario 1: Account Creation and First Impression

**Task:** "You want to use Bidinly to buy a property. Start by creating an account."

**Steps:**
1. Open the homepage
2. Navigate to sign up
3. Create a Buyer account
4. Log in

**Observe:**
- [ ] Is it clear what Bidinly is about?
- [ ] Is the sign-up process easy to understand?
- [ ] Are Buyer and Agent roles clear?
- [ ] Do error messages make sense?
- [ ] Can they complete sign-up without help?
- [ ] Do they understand what happens after logging in?

**Follow-up Questions:**
- What was your first impression of the homepage?
- Was anything confusing during sign-up?
- Did you understand what you could do after logging in?
- Was the difference between Buyer and Agent roles clear?

---

### Scenario 2: Browsing Properties and Placing a Bid

**Task:** "Find a property you like and place a bid on it."

**Steps:**
1. Browse or search for properties
2. Apply basic filters (city, bedrooms, bathrooms, size)
3. Open a property detail page
4. Review property information
5. Place a bid (with amount, deal, and expiry date)
6. Check that the bid appears in your bids list

**Observe:**
- [ ] Can the user find and use the search bar?
- [ ] Do they understand how filters work?
- [ ] Is the property information clear and complete?
- [ ] Is the bidding process understandable?
- [ ] Do they understand what "Deal" means in the bid form?
- [ ] Do they understand bid expiry date?
- [ ] Can they easily navigate to their bids?
- [ ] Is the bid status clear?

**Follow-up Questions:**
- How easy was it to find a suitable property?
- Was placing a bid clear and intuitive?
- What information was missing, if any?
- Did you understand what the "Deal" field was for?
- How clear was the bid expiry date concept?
- Was the property detail page helpful?

---

### Scenario 3: Managing Bids

**Task:** "Review all your bids and see which ones have been accepted, rejected, or are still pending."

**Steps:**
1. Navigate to your bids page
2. Review bid statuses
3. Try filtering bids (by ID, property name, status)
4. Try deleting a bid if needed

**Observe:**
- [ ] Is navigation to bids easy?
- [ ] Are statuses clear and distinguishable?
- [ ] Is the table layout readable?
- [ ] Do they understand what each status means?
- [ ] Can they effectively use filters?
- [ ] Is the delete action clear?

**Follow-up Questions:**
- Was it easy to understand your bid statuses?
- What would you improve on this page?
- Were the filters useful?
- Was there any information you expected but didn't see?

---

### Scenario 4: Navigation and Notifications

**Task:** "Navigate around the platform and check for any notifications."

**Steps:**
1. Use the navigation menu to move between pages
2. Check notifications (if available)
3. Try logging out and logging back in

**Observe:**
- [ ] Is the navigation menu clear?
- [ ] Do they understand where each link goes?
- [ ] Is the user dropdown menu intuitive?
- [ ] Are notifications easy to access?
- [ ] Does navigation feel smooth?

**Follow-up Questions:**
- How intuitive was the navigation?
- Did you always know where you were in the application?
- Was the user menu easy to use?
